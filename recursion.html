What Recursion Means to me

To me recursion is the idea of something telling itself to do something.

Examples of Recursion

A good example of recursion is Fractals which by definition are recursive. Fractals are mathematical which contain a smaller version of themself. One of these fractals is Sierpiński's Triangle.

S-S-S Strategy

The S-S-S Strategy is a way of visualizing the recusion of something. The steps of the strategy simplify to:
  • Simplify the function to more easily understand it
  • Substiute the base case with its value
  • Solve in reverse. Start from the end and work back to the start
For my code:
  • Simplified, my code take a single triangle and splits it into three
  • The base case for my code is when the triangle has been split 8 times
  • Solved in reverse, you would take a completly split triangle and turn each of the sets of three triangles into one larger triangle

Divide and Conquer Strategy

Another strategy for understanding recursion is to imagine it like a tree with your starting function as the base of the tree and everytime a function is called that becomes a different branch.
This can be very useful for visualizing the path a program can take and the performance effects of the recursion.
